# Layer Structure — Final vs. Working

Chronikon uses a simple, two-part workflow:

- `manuscript/` is the protected, holy version of the story.
- Everything outside `manuscript/` is working material: notes, fragments, worldbuilding, backstory, scene seeds, and structural experiments.

## Manuscript is the final text

The files under `manuscript/chapters/` are the story as it currently stands.

- Keep only the text you want to count, build, and preserve as the current draft.
- Move prose into this folder only once it feels ready to be part of the narrative.
- Treat `manuscript/` as the clean, assembled, protected version of Chronikon.
- Do not edit `manuscript/` on a whim. Every change here should be intentional and committed.

## The rest is sticky-note space

The other folders are deliberately loose.

- `mindmap/` is the incubator between Theory and Reference: a hyperlinked worldbuilding steam room for connections, fragments, and emergent ideas.
- `principles/` is for frameworks, themes, structural ideas, and story logic.
- `reference/` is for research, scripture anchors, artwork, soundtrack mappings, and immutable source material.
- `workflow/` is for outlines, scene lists, backstory fragments, draft rules, and process notes.
- `PROGRESS.md` is for tracking where the manuscript currently stands.

This means:

- You can move scenes, backstory, and worldbuilding around freely outside `manuscript/`.
- You can draft scenes as short notes, bullet lists, or loose paragraphs.
- You can gather ideas in any order, then recombine them into the manuscript when you are ready.

## Recommended habit

1. Capture every scene idea, character sketch, and worldbuilding note outside `manuscript/` first.
2. Use `workflow/`, `principles/`, or `reference/` for scratch work and loose organization.
3. When a fragment is ready, copy it into `manuscript/chapters/` as a chapter file.
4. Keep the manuscript order intentional; the rest of the repo can stay messy.

That way, the novel has one clean home while the rest of the workspace stays flexible and easy to rearrange.
